**Prepositions** — all **basic terms and examples** — especially useful for **spoken English**. 💬

---

## 🌟 What is a Preposition?

👉 A **preposition** is a word that shows the **relationship** between a noun or pronoun and another word in a sentence.

It usually tells **time, place, direction, cause, possession, or manner.**

### Example:

- The book is **on** the table.  
    → (“on” shows place)
    
- She arrived **at** 5 o’clock.  
    → (“at” shows time)
    

---

## 🧭 1. Prepositions of **Place / Position**

| Preposition         | Meaning                   | Example                                |
| ------------------- | ------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| **in**              | inside something          | She is **in** the room.                |
| **on**              | on top of a surface       | The phone is **on** the table.         |
| **at**              | specific point/place      | I am **at** the station.               |
| **under**           | below something           | The cat is **under** the bed.          |
| **above**           | higher but not touching   | The fan is **above** my head.          |
| **over**            | covering or moving across | The plane flew **over** the city.      |
| **behind**          | at the back of            | He is **behind** the door.             |
| **in front of**     | before something          | The car is **in front of** the house.  |
| **between**         | in the middle of two      | The school is **between** two shops.   |
| **among**           | in the middle of many     | She was **among** her friends.         |
| **near / close to** | not far from              | The bank is **near** my home.          |
| **opposite**        | facing something          | The park is **opposite** the hospital. |

## ⏰ 2. Prepositions of **Time**

| Preposition    | Used for                             | Example                            |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------- |
| **at**         | exact time                           | I wake up **at** 7 a.m.            |
| **on**         | days and dates                       | My birthday is **on** Monday.      |
| **in**         | months, years, seasons, long periods | I was born **in** 2005.            |
| **before**     | earlier than                         | She came **before** me.            |
| **after**      | later than                           | We will go **after** lunch.        |
| **since**      | starting time (till now)             | I have lived here **since** 2020.  |
| **for**        | duration of time                     | I’ve been waiting **for** 2 hours. |
| **during**     | within a period                      | She slept **during** the movie.    |
| **by**         | not later than                       | Finish the work **by** 5 p.m.      |
| **until/till** | up to a certain time                 | Stay here **until** I come.        |

## 🚶‍♀️ 3. Prepositions of **Direction / Movement**

| Preposition | Meaning                            | Example                                |
| ----------- | ---------------------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| **to**      | movement toward                    | She is going **to** school.            |
| **into**    | going inside                       | He jumped **into** the pool.           |
| **out of**  | leaving                            | She ran **out of** the room.           |
| **from**    | starting point                     | He came **from** London.               |
| **towards** | in the direction of                | The cat walked **towards** me.         |
| **through** | from one side to another           | The car passed **through** the tunnel. |
| **across**  | from one side to another (surface) | They swam **across** the river.        |
| **along**   | following a line/path              | They walked **along** the beach.       |
| **up**      | towards higher place               | He climbed **up** the hill.            |
| **down**    | towards lower place                | The ball rolled **down** the stairs.   |

---

## 🎯 4. Prepositions of **Cause, Reason, Purpose**

| Preposition                | Example                                 |
| -------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| **for** – purpose          | He works **for** his family.            |
| **because of** – reason    | He failed **because of** laziness.      |
| **due to** – reason        | The match was canceled **due to** rain. |
| **from** – cause or source | He suffers **from** fever.              |

---

## 👤 5. Prepositions of **Possession**

| Preposition | Example                             |
| ----------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **of**      | The color **of** the sky is blue.   |
| **with**    | The man **with** a hat is my uncle. |
| **without** | Don’t go **without** permission.    |

---

## 🧩 6. Prepositions of **Manner / Method**

| Preposition                | Example                      |
| -------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| **by** – method            | I travel **by** bus.         |
| **with** – instrument      | She cut it **with** a knife. |
| **like / as** – comparison | He runs **like** a cheetah.  |

---

## 🗣️ 7. Common Prepositional Phrases for **Spoken English**

| Phrase                                   | Meaning                | Example                      |
| ---------------------------------------- | ---------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| **in the morning / evening / afternoon** | time                   | I study **in the morning**.  |
| **at night / at noon / at midnight**     | specific time          | I sleep **at night**.        |
| **on time / in time**                    | punctual / before time | I reached **on time**.       |
| **in front of**                          | before something       | Sit **in front of** me.      |
| **in a hurry**                           | quickly                | He left **in a hurry**.      |
| **by mistake**                           | accidentally           | I called you **by mistake**. |
| **on purpose**                           | intentionally          | He broke it **on purpose**.  |
