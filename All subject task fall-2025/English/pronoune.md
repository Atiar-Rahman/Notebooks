**Pronouns** in detail — an essential part of English grammar for speaking and writing correctly.

---

## 🧠 **What is a Pronoun?**

A **pronoun** is a word that replaces a **noun** to avoid repetition.

### Example:

- Without pronoun: **`Rina`** is my friend. **`Rina`** is very smart.
    
- With pronoun: **`Rina`** is my friend. **She** is very smart.
    

Here, **she** replaces “`Rina`”.

---

## 🏷️ **Types of Pronouns (with Examples)**

### 1. **Personal Pronouns**

Used instead of names of people or things.

|Type|Subject Form|Object Form|Example|
|---|---|---|---|
|1st person|I, we|me, us|I like tea. / She gave **us** coffee.|
|2nd person|you|you|**You** are my friend.|
|3rd person|he, she, it, they|him, her, it, them|**He** is tall. / I know **them**.|

---

### 2. **Possessive Pronouns**

Show ownership or possession.

|Type|Examples|Example in sentence|
|---|---|---|
|Possessive adjectives|my, your, his, her, its, our, their|This is **my** book.|
|Possessive pronouns|mine, yours, his, hers, ours, theirs|This book is **mine**.|

---

### 3. **Reflexive Pronouns**

Used when the subject and the object are the same person.

|Pronoun|Example|
|---|---|
|myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves|He hurt **himself**. / I enjoyed **myself**.|

---

### 4. **Demonstrative Pronouns**

Used to point out people or things.

|Pronoun|Use|Example|
|---|---|---|
|this, that, these, those|to point something|**This** is my pen. / **Those** are apples.|

---

### 5. **Interrogative Pronouns**

Used to ask questions.

|Pronoun|Example|
|---|---|
|who, whom, whose, which, what|**Who** is there? / **What** is your name?|

---

### 6. **Relative Pronouns**

Used to join two sentences and refer to a noun mentioned earlier.

|Pronoun|Example|
|---|---|
|who, whom, whose, which, that|The man **who** came here is my uncle. / The car **that** I bought is red.|

---

### 7. **Indefinite Pronouns**

Refer to people or things in a general way.

|Pronoun|Example|
|---|---|
|someone, anyone, everyone, nobody, nothing, each, few, many, all, some|**Everyone** likes music. / **Few** were present.|

---

### 8. **Reciprocal Pronouns**

Show a mutual relationship.

|Pronoun|Example|
|---|---|
|each other, one another|They love **each other**.|

---

### 9. **Distributive Pronouns**

Refer to persons or things one at a time.

|Pronoun|Example|
|---|---|
|each, either, neither|**Each** of the boys has a pen. / **Neither** is correct.|

---

## 💬 **Quick Tip for Spoken English**

In daily speech:

- Use **he/she/they** instead of repeating names.
    
- Use **this** and **that** to point to things nearby or far.
    
- Use **yourself/myself** politely: “Please help yourself.”
