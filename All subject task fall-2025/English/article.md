Let’s learn about **“a,” “an,” and “the”** — they are called **articles** in English grammar. 🌿

---

## 📝 **Articles in English Grammar**

Articles are **words used before nouns** to show whether we are talking about something **specific** or **general**.  
There are **two types of articles**:

1. **Indefinite Articles** → _a_, _an_
    
2. **Definite Article** → _the_
    

---

### 🔹 **1. Indefinite Articles: “a” and “an”**

These are used when we talk about **something general**, not a specific person or thing.

- **“A”** is used before words that begin with a **consonant sound**.  
    👉 Example:
    
    - a cat
        
    - a book
        
    - a university _(starts with the sound “yu”)_
        
- **“An”** is used before words that begin with a **vowel sound (a, e, i, o, u)**.  
    👉 Example:
    
    - an apple
        
    - an egg
        
    - an honest man _(the “h” is silent, so it sounds like a vowel)_
        
    - an hour
        

🟢 **Use “a/an” when mentioning something for the first time.**  
Example:

- I saw **a** dog in the street.
    
- She ate **an** orange this morning.
    

---

### 🔹 **2. Definite Article: “the”**

“The” is used when we talk about **a specific** person, place, or thing — something the listener already knows.

👉 Example:

- The sun rises in the east.
    
- Please close **the** door.
    
- I met **the** teacher you talked about.
    
- **The** Earth revolves around the Sun.
    

🟢 **Use “the” when both speaker and listener know what is being talked about.**

---

### 🔸 **Quick Comparison**

|Situation|Example|
|---|---|
|Talking about something for the first time|I saw **a** bird.|
|Talking about it again|**The** bird was beautiful.|
|Before consonant sound|**a** pen|
|Before vowel sound|**an** umbrella|
|Specific or unique thing|**The** Moon, **The** President|

---

### 🌟 **Summary**

- **“A/An”** → Used for general things (not known to the listener).
    
- **“The”** → Used for specific things (known or unique).
