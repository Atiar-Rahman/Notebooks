In English grammar, **verbs** are divided into several **types** based on their **function and meaning**. Here’s a clear breakdown 👇

---

### 🧩 **Main Types of Verbs**

#### **1. Main (Lexical) Verbs**

These express the **main action or state** in a sentence.  
👉 _Examples:_ run, eat, go, think, sleep  
**Example Sentence:**

- She **runs** every morning.
    
- He **knows** the answer.
    

---

#### **2. Auxiliary (Helping) Verbs**

These help the main verb form **tenses, questions, negatives, and voices**.  
👉 _Examples:_ be, have, do  
**Example Sentence:**

- She **is running**.
    
- They **have finished**.
    
- I **do not know**.
    

---

#### **3. Modal Verbs**

These show **possibility, ability, permission, or obligation**.  
👉 _Examples:_ can, could, may, might, shall, should, will, would, must  
**Example Sentence:**

- You **must** study hard.
    
- I **can** swim.
    

---

### 🧠 **Other Classifications**

#### **4. Transitive Verbs**

Need an **object** to complete their meaning.  
👉 _Example:_ I **read** a book.  
(“a book” is the object)

#### **5. Intransitive Verbs**

Do **not need** an object.  
👉 _Example:_ He **sleeps**.

---

#### **6. Linking (Copular) Verbs**

Link the **subject** to more information about it.  
👉 _Examples:_ be, seem, become, appear, look, feel  
**Example:** She **is** happy.

---

#### **7. Regular Verbs**

Form past tense by adding **–ed**.  
👉 _Examples:_ play → played, walk → walked

#### **8. Irregular Verbs**

Change form completely in the past tense.  
👉 _Examples:_ go → went, eat → ate, come → came

---

#### **9. Finite Verbs**

Change according to the **subject and tense**.  
👉 _Example:_ He **goes** to school.

#### **10. Non-finite Verbs**

Do **not change** according to subject or tense.  
👉 _Examples:_ to go (infinitive), going (gerund), gone (participle)

---

# Perfect 👍 Let’s go **deep into all types of verbs** — explained **clearly + with plenty of examples** so you understand and remember easily.

---

## 🧩 **VERBS — Detailed Explanation with Examples**

A **verb** is a word that expresses an **action**, **state**, or **occurrence**.  
Example:

- She **runs** fast.
    
- He **is** a doctor.
    
- The glass **broke**.
    

---

## 🔹 **1. Main (Lexical) Verbs**

Main verbs tell us the **main action or state** of the subject.  
They can stand **alone** or with **helping verbs**.

**Examples:**

- I **play** football.
    
- They **study** English.
    
- He **thinks** deeply.
    
- She **knows** the answer.
    

---

## 🔹 **2. Auxiliary (Helping) Verbs**

These verbs help the **main verb** form different **tenses, questions, negatives, and voices**.

### ➤ Types of Auxiliary Verbs:

#### a) **Primary Auxiliaries:**

**Be, Have, Do**

**Examples:**

- She **is reading**. → (be + main verb)
    
- They **have finished** the work. → (have + past participle)
    
- I **do not like** coffee. → (do + not + main verb)
    

#### b) **Modal Auxiliaries:**

**Can, Could, May, Might, Shall, Should, Will, Would, Must, Ought to, Need, Dare**

**Examples:**

- I **can** swim. (ability)
    
- You **must** go now. (obligation)
    
- He **might** come tomorrow. (possibility)
    
- We **should** study. (advice)
    

---

## 🔹 **3. Modal Verbs (Detailed)**

Modal verbs express **mood, possibility, ability, permission, or necessity**.

|Modal|Use|Example|
|---|---|---|
|can|ability|She **can** dance.|
|could|past ability / polite request|**Could** you help me?|
|may|permission / possibility|You **may** go now.|
|might|weaker possibility|It **might** rain.|
|must|obligation / strong necessity|You **must** wear a helmet.|
|shall|future / offer|We **shall** meet again.|
|should|advice / duty|You **should** study more.|
|will|future / willingness|I **will** call you.|
|would|polite / imaginary|I **would** go if I had time.|
|ought to|moral duty|You **ought to** respect elders.|
|need to|necessity|You **need to** work hard.|
|dare|courage|He **dare not** speak.|

---

## 🔹 **4. Transitive Verbs**

Transitive verbs **need an object** to complete their meaning.

**Examples:**

- I **read** a book.
    
- She **plays** the guitar.
    
- He **bought** a car.
    

👉 If you remove the object, the sentence becomes incomplete:  
❌ I read — (what?)

---

## 🔹 **5. Intransitive Verbs**

Intransitive verbs **do not need an object**.  
They express a **complete action** by themselves.

**Examples:**

- He **sleeps**.
    
- The baby **cried**.
    
- The sun **rises**.
    

✅ Sentence complete without an object.

---

## 🔹 **6. Linking (Copular) Verbs**

They **link the subject** to additional information (a noun/adjective).  
They don’t show action — they show **state or condition**.

**Common Linking Verbs:**  
**be, become, seem, appear, feel, look, taste, sound, grow**

**Examples:**

- She **is** happy.
    
- He **became** angry.
    
- The soup **tastes** delicious.
    
- You **look** tired.
    

---

## 🔹 **7. Regular Verbs**

These verbs form their **past** and **past participle** by adding **–ed** or **–d**.

|Present|Past|Past Participle|
|---|---|---|
|work|worked|worked|
|play|played|played|
|watch|watched|watched|
|clean|cleaned|cleaned|

**Examples:**

- I **worked** yesterday.
    
- She **played** football.
    

---

## 🔹 **8. Irregular Verbs**

These verbs **change form** completely in past and past participle.

|Present|Past|Past Participle|
|---|---|---|
|go|went|gone|
|eat|ate|eaten|
|come|came|come|
|write|wrote|written|
|see|saw|seen|

**Examples:**

- He **went** to school.
    
- I have **written** a letter.
    

---

## 🔹 **9. Finite Verbs**

A **finite verb** changes form according to **subject** and **tense**.

**Examples:**

- He **goes** to school.
    
- They **go** to school.
    
- She **was** happy.
    

👉 "goes" → changes with subject “he”.  
👉 "was" → changes with tense (past).

---

## 🔹 **10. Non-Finite Verbs**

Non-finite verbs **do not change** with subject or tense.  
There are **3 types**:

### a) **Infinitives:**

Base form of the verb, usually with **to**.  
**Examples:**

- I want **to eat**.
    
- She likes **to sing**.
    

### b) **Gerunds:**

“–ing” form used as a **noun**.  
**Examples:**

- **Reading** is fun.
    
- She enjoys **dancing**.
    

### c) **Participles:**

Used as adjectives or in verb phrases.  
**Present Participle (–ing):**

- The **crying** baby needs milk.  
    **Past Participle (–ed / irregular form):**
    
- The **broken** glass is on the floor.
    

---

## 🔹 **11. Dynamic Verbs**

Show **an action that can start and stop**.

**Examples:**

- run, jump, eat, write
    
- He **runs** every day.
    

---

## 🔹 **12. Stative Verbs**

Show **a state or condition**, not an action.  
They are usually **not used in continuous tenses**.

**Examples:**

- know, believe, love, hate, own, seem  
    **Example:**
    
- I **know** the answer.
    
- She **owns** a car.
    

❌ Not: _I am knowing the answer._

---

## 🔹 **13. Phrasal Verbs**

These are verbs + prepositions/adverbs that together make **a new meaning**.

**Examples:**

- **look after** (take care of) → She **looks after** her mother.
    
- **give up** (stop) → He **gave up** smoking.
    
- **run out of** (use up) → We **ran out of** milk.
    
- **bring up** (raise) → She **brought up** two children.
