
### 🔹 **Definition:**

A **sentence** is a group of words that **expresses a complete thought**.  
It always has a **subject** (who/what the sentence is about) and a **predicate** (what is said about the subject).  
It also **starts with a capital letter** and **ends with a full stop (.)**, **question mark (?)**, or **exclamation mark (!)**.

---

### 🔹 **Structure of a Sentence:**

A sentence usually has two main parts:

1. **Subject** → The person or thing performing the action.
    
2. **Predicate** → The part that tells what the subject does or is.
    

📘 **Example:**  
👉 _Rafi plays football._

- **Subject:** Rafi
    
- **Predicate:** plays football
    

---

### 🔹 **Types of Sentences (by Function):**

1. **Declarative Sentence (Statement)**  
    ➤ Used to give information or state a fact.  
    ✅ _Example:_ I am learning English.
    
2. **Interrogative Sentence (Question)**  
    ➤ Used to ask a question.  
    ✅ _Example:_ Are you learning English?
    
3. **Imperative Sentence (Command or Request)**  
    ➤ Used to give an order or make a request.  
    ✅ _Example:_ Please close the door.
    
4. **Exclamatory Sentence (Strong Feeling)**  
    ➤ Used to express emotions like surprise, joy, anger, etc.  
    ✅ _Example:_ What a beautiful day it is!
    

---

### 🔹 **Types of Sentences (by Structure):**

1. **Simple Sentence** – One independent clause.  
    ✅ _Example:_ I like apples.
    
2. **Compound Sentence** – Two or more independent clauses joined by _and, but, or, so, yet._  
    ✅ _Example:_ I like apples, and I also like bananas.
    
3. **Complex Sentence** – One independent + one or more dependent clauses.  
    ✅ _Example:_ I like apples because they are sweet.
    
4. **Compound-Complex Sentence** – Two or more independent + one or more dependent clauses.  
    ✅ _Example:_ I like apples because they are sweet, and I eat them every day.
    

---

### 🔹 **Rules for Writing a Correct Sentence:**

1. Must have a **subject and predicate**.
    
2. Must **express a complete thought**.
    
3. Must begin with a **capital letter**.
    
4. Must end with **proper punctuation** (., ?, !).
    
5. Must have **proper grammar and structure**.
