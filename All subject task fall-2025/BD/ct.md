
## **1. What are the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)? Discuss their purpose and importance.**

The **Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)** are a set of **17 global goals** adopted by the **United Nations (UN) in 2015**.  
These goals aim to **end poverty**, **protect the environment**, and **ensure peace and prosperity for all people** by **2030**.

### **Purpose of SDGs**

- To create a **better and fairer world**
    
- To ensure **economic growth without harming nature**
    
- To improve **quality of life for present and future generations**
    

### **Importance of SDGs**

- Reduce poverty and hunger
    
- Promote education, health, and gender equality
    
- Protect environment and fight climate change
    
- Encourage global cooperation
    

👉 **In short**, SDGs guide countries toward **sustainable and balanced development**.

## **2. Describe the main objectives of the Sustainable Development Goals.**

The main objectives of the SDGs are to solve major global problems affecting humanity and the planet.

### **Key Objectives**

- **End poverty and hunger** worldwide
    
- Ensure **quality education and good health**
    
- Promote **gender equality and women empowerment**
    
- Provide **clean water and sanitation**
    
- Ensure **affordable and clean energy**
    
- Promote **decent work and economic growth**
    
- Reduce **inequality within and among countries**
    
- Protect the **environment and climate**
    
- Build **peaceful and inclusive societies**
    

👉 These objectives aim to achieve **social, economic, and environmental sustainability**.

---

## **3. Explain why the United Nations introduced the SDGs in 2015.**

The United Nations introduced the SDGs in **2015** to address the **unfinished and emerging global challenges**.

### **Reasons for Introducing SDGs**

- Many countries still faced **poverty, inequality, and unemployment**
    
- Environmental problems like **climate change** were increasing
    
- Previous goals (MDGs) were **limited in scope**
    
- Need for a **universal plan** applicable to all countries
    
- To ensure **long-term sustainable development**
    

👉 Therefore, SDGs were introduced as a **comprehensive global agenda** for a better future by 2030.

## **4. Why is global partnership and cooperation important for achieving the SDGs?**

Global partnership and cooperation are important because **no single country can achieve the SDGs alone**.

### **Importance of Global Partnership**

- Sharing **technology and knowledge**
    
- Providing **financial support** to poor countries
    
- Solving **global problems** like climate change together
    
- Strengthening **international peace and stability**
    
- Helping countries learn from **successful models**
    

👉 **SDG-17** specifically focuses on **Partnership for the Goals**, showing its importance.

---

## **5. Why are the SDGs important for developing countries like Bangladesh? Explain.**

The SDGs are very important for developing countries like **Bangladesh** because they address key national challenges.

### **Importance for Bangladesh**

- Help reduce **poverty and unemployment**
    
- Improve **education and healthcare**
    
- Support **climate change adaptation** (floods, cyclones)
    
- Promote **women empowerment**
    
- Encourage **sustainable economic growth**
    
- Attract **foreign aid and investment**
    

👉 SDGs help Bangladesh achieve **Vision 2041** and ensure inclusive development.

---

## **6. Explain the role of global cooperation in achieving the Sustainable Development Goals.**

Global cooperation plays a crucial role in achieving the SDGs by bringing countries together.

### **Role of Global Cooperation**

- Joint action against **climate change**
    
- Financial assistance to **least developed countries**
    
- Transfer of **modern technology**
    
- Support during **natural disasters and pandemics**
    
- Promoting peace and conflict resolution
    

👉 Without global cooperation, achieving the SDGs by **2030 is impossible**.
