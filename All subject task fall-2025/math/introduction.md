
### **📘 Introduction to Statistics**

**Statistics** is the branch of mathematics that deals with **collecting, organizing, analyzing, interpreting, and presenting data**.  
It helps us **make decisions** or **draw conclusions** from data — instead of relying only on guesses or opinions.

In simple words:

> **Statistics turns data into useful information.**

---

### **⚙️ Two Main Types of Statistics**

1. **Descriptive Statistics:**
    
    - Summarizes or describes data.
        
    - Examples: mean (average), median, mode, standard deviation, graphs, and charts.
        
    - Example use: “The average height of students in a class is 165 cm.”
        
2. **Inferential Statistics:**
    
    - Uses sample data to make predictions or conclusions about a larger group (population).
        
    - Example use: “Based on a survey of 500 people, we estimate that 60% of the city supports the new policy.”
        

---

### **📊 Where Statistics is Used (Applications)**

1. **Education:**
    
    - Analyzing student performance, test results, and school rankings.
        
2. **Business and Marketing:**
    
    - Understanding customer behavior, sales trends, and market research.
        
3. **Healthcare and Medicine:**
    
    - Evaluating treatment effectiveness, clinical trials, and disease patterns.
        
4. **Government and Policy:**
    
    - Conducting census surveys, unemployment rate analysis, and policy evaluation.
        
5. **Science and Research:**
    
    - Designing experiments, analyzing results, and validating hypotheses.
        
6. **Sports:**
    
    - Tracking player performance and predicting match outcomes.
        
7. **Data Science and Machine Learning:**
    
    - Used in data analysis, model building, and performance evaluation.

|Concept|Purpose|Example|
