 **Data Mining / `KDD`**, there are **different types of knowledge discovery processes**, depending on the task we want to perform.

---

# 🔹 **Types of Knowledge Discovery Processes in Data Mining**

### 1. **Classification (Supervised Learning)**

- **Goal**: Assign items to predefined categories (labels).
    
- **Input**: Training data with known labels.
    
- **Output**: A model that predicts categories for new data.
    
- **Example**: Email classification → spam vs. not spam.
    

---

### 2. **Regression (Supervised Learning)**

- **Goal**: Predict continuous numerical values.
    
- **Input**: Data with input-output pairs.
    
- **Output**: A regression model (linear, polynomial, etc.).
    
- **Example**: Predicting house prices based on size and location.
    

---

### 3. **Clustering (Unsupervised Learning)**

- **Goal**: Group similar data points together without predefined labels.
    
- **Input**: Unlabeled data.
    
- **Output**: Clusters (groups of similar objects).
    
- **Example**: Market segmentation → grouping customers by purchasing behavior.
    

---

### 4. **Association Rule Mining (Dependency Modeling)**

- **Goal**: Discover relationships between items in large datasets.
    
- **Input**: Transactional data.
    
- **Output**: Rules like _“If A is bought, B is also likely to be bought.”_
    
- **Example**: Market basket analysis → “People who buy bread often buy butter.”
    

---

### 5. **Anomaly/Outlier Detection**

- **Goal**: Detect unusual or rare patterns in data.
    
- **Input**: Data with mostly normal patterns.
    
- **Output**: Identification of unusual data points.
    
- **Example**: Fraud detection in credit card transactions.
    

---

### 6. **Summarization**

- **Goal**: Provide a compact representation of the dataset.
    
- **Input**: Large dataset.
    
- **Output**: Summary, visualization, or descriptive statistics.
    
- **Example**: Generating monthly sales reports or dashboards.
    

---

### 7. **Sequential / Temporal Pattern Mining**

- **Goal**: Discover patterns over time or sequence.
    
- **Input**: Time-series or sequential data.
    
- **Output**: Patterns showing order of events.
    
- **Example**: Web clickstream analysis → “Users often visit page A, then B, then C.”
    

---

# ✅ **In Short**

The main **types of knowledge discovery in data mining** are:

1. Classification
    
2. Regression
    
3. Clustering
    
4. Association Rule Mining
    
5. Anomaly Detection
    
6. Summarization
    
7. Sequential Pattern Mining
